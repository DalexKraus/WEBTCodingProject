What are self driving cars?

Self driving cars are vehicles that are capable of driving without any human influence or input.
They have Sensor that check their environment.

If there are any obstacles around them, they try to find a way not to hit them.
Most self driving cars today are electric cars as well. But whether the vehicle is electric or not, the technique works mostly the same.
Electric cars are just more modern than cars with gasoline.

But there are also hybrid cars which use both - gasoline and electricity.

There are 5 different levels of automation:

  1. Driver assistance: Small assisting things like automatic acceleration or something like this. But: everything is under Human control!
  2. Partial Automation: This is what some of the Tesla Vehicles have. The car can also automatically take safety actions. But still, a human has to stay alert at the wheel.
  3. Conditional automation: This level still requires a human driver, but the human is able to put some “safety-critical functions” to the vehicle, under certain traffic or environmental conditions. But: This poses some safety issues because the human driver passes the major task of driving to or from the car itself.
  4. High automation: A car that can drive itself almost all the time without any human input, but might be programmed not to drive in unmapped areas or during severe weather. This is a car you could sleep in.
  5. Full automation: This means full automation in every condition. This does have much less safety issues than any other form of driving.
